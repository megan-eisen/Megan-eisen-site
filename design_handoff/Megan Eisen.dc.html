The person you'd work with

Megan – at work anywhere in the world.

I read a business by where it drags, not where it hurts. A customer-experience problem is usually an ownership problem. A drifting roadmap is usually a revenue problem. An AI ambition is usually a data-and-identity problem – most companies want AI features; far fewer have built the foundation AI actually runs on. My job is to find the layer creating the drag and rebuild what the business has outgrown – not by adding process, but by making the system visible enough that decisions move again.

I start with the business itself – strategy, org design, how decisions get made, and the budget that funds them. Then the experience: what the customer, user, or guest actually meets. Then the product and the craft that delivers it. Most executives own one of the three. I've led all three, and I hold them together exactly when scale is pulling them apart.

Megan – as a human.

I've moved across the world with two suitcases, which is roughly how I work: light, fast, and useful anywhere within weeks. I design my life the way I design an operating model – deliberately, around what actually matters, with nothing carried that doesn't earn its place. Experiences over things. Ownership over titles. Time and location on my own terms.

I've explored 29 countries across five continents with my wife, and we are always mapping the next one. I love the wind in my hair, sunshine, fresh air, alpine cheese, exploring cultures different than mine. I'm inspired by the creativity of humans and being "Auntie" makes me happy. I'm into working with hands, growing and building are my happy place, plating nightly is my creativity. My Swiss-Mexican wife is my everything.
